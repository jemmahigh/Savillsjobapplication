02 / Observations

What I noticed
about Savills

01
Unmatched Brand Prestige
The Savills name carries enormous weight. Heritage, expertise and global reach are all baked in. The marketing role here is about making sure that prestige shows up consistently everywhere the brand lives online.
02
Strong Instagram Foundation
263K followers is a serious base to build from. The feed is polished and aspirational. The opportunity is to go deeper on storytelling and turn followers into genuine enquiries rather than passive admirers.
03
TikTok is Wide Open
28.5K TikTok followers versus 263K on Instagram tells a clear story. The younger generation of buyers and renters is almost entirely on TikTok. That gap is not a weakness, it is the single biggest growth opportunity available right now.
04
Content That Builds Authority
Savills produces world class research and market reports. That intellectual capital is enormously valuable as content. Turning it into short, shareable, human formats is where I would start to close the gap between brand awareness and active engagement.
03 / Audience

Who Savills
speaks to

Savills has a broad and varied audience. Great marketing does not try to speak to everyone the same way. It speaks to each group in a way that feels made for them.

3 Audiences
Each with different motivations and channels
1 Brand Voice
Consistent, confident and authoritative across all of them
Digital First
Every audience now finds Savills through a screen first
Established Wealth and Investors
High net worth individuals, portfolio landlords, institutional buyers and international purchasers. They expect total professionalism, deep expertise and a brand that matches their standards. LinkedIn, thought leadership and premium editorial content speak directly to this group.
Aspirational and First Time Premium Buyers
The next generation of Savills clients. Professionals in their late 20s and 30s making their first significant property move. They are on Instagram and TikTok. They are curious, research driven and highly influenced by content that feels real and honest.
Commercial and Corporate Clients
Businesses looking for office space, developers and institutional real estate decision makers. They respond to data, market intelligence and a brand that demonstrates it understands their world. Savills research output is the most powerful content tool here.

06 / Lead Generation

Marketing that
drives enquiries

Every piece of content should have a clear path from curiosity to conversation. This is how I would build that journey for Savills.

01
Valuable Hook Content
Start with content that earns attention before asking for anything. Market insight posts, property showcases and expert commentary that makes someone think Savills actually understands my world.
All Channels
02
SEO and Editorial Content
Savills research is world class. Turning it into blog posts, guides and property market explainers builds organic traffic and positions the brand as the go to source of property intelligence. Long term, this is the most valuable lead channel available.
Website + Blog
03
Email Nurture Sequences
A warm, well timed email sequence turns a passive enquiry into an active conversation. Market updates, curated properties and a personal touch from an advisor. Nurturing takes time but produces the highest quality leads.
Email
04
Retarget and Convert
Follow up with everyone who showed intent but did not take action. A well targeted ad showing the exact property they looked at, paired with a simple booking prompt, closes the loop between browsing and booking a viewing.
Paid Social
Measure everything: organic traffic growth · enquiry rate by channel · cost per lead · email open rates · conversion from viewing to instruction
